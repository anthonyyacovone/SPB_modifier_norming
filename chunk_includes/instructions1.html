INSTRUCTIONS

In this experiment, you will read sentences that describe children playing with various toys. Your job is to rate the plausibility of each sentence.

How should I rate 'plausibility'?

You will have a 1-7 scale that goes from 'Very Implausible' (1) to 'Very Plausible' (7). Plausibility should reflect how likely the described event is to actually happen given the context of children playing with a set of toys. 

What kind of toys do the children have?

The children described in the sentences have various stuffed animals (e.g. frogs, leopards) and both big and small objects (e.g. candle, feather, tiny candle, tiny feather). The image below shows a typical set of toys that the children can play with in this game.

 

Some example sentences and ratings based on the set of toys above:

Sentence: Jane hugged the frog that has a feather!   Rating: Very plausible (7)
Sentence: Jimmy licked the leopard!  Rating:  Less plausible (5)
Sentence: Kim drank the candle!  Rating: Very implausible (1)

Will I see pictures like the one above? 

During the experiment, you will not see pictures of the toys that the child played with. However, all of the toys decribed will be stuffed animals and big/small objects like the ones above! 

You will read some practice sentences in order to get used to the rating scale! You can either click the box with your rating or simply hit the number key on your keyboard.
